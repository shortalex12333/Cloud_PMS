# `brand-refusal-list.md`

## CelesteOS — Brand Refusal List (Non-Negotiable)

### Purpose

This is the shortest, hardest brand document we have.

It exists to stop drift.

If anything in product, marketing, sales, or design violates a refusal below, it is rejected immediately.

No debate. No “just this once”.

---

# 1) We refuse to be a chatbot

We will never:

* present Celeste as a conversational companion
* use friendly or emotional tone in-product
* write “I think”, “I believe”, “don’t worry”, “hang tight”
* include personality, jokes, or reassurance

Celeste is an instrument.

---

# 2) We refuse AI theatre

We will never:

* say “AI-powered”, “smart”, “intelligent assistant”, “next-gen”
* use “confidence scores” as marketing
* show reasoning essays or “why we think this”
* design visuals that suggest “brain / mind / thinking”

We show sources and evidence. That’s it.

---

# 3) We refuse silent behavior

We will never:

* auto-execute actions
* mutate data in the background
* hide side effects
* imply a change occurred before it is committed

If something changes, the user sees:
Preview → Sign → Commit → Record.

---

# 4) We refuse to collapse uncertainty

We will never:

* guess on behalf of the user
* auto-select “best match” when ambiguity exists
* hide lower-confidence interpretations

Uncertainty is structured and visible.

---

# 5) We refuse SaaS UX patterns

We will never:

* build a navigation-first product
* lead with dashboards
* add modules and menus for “power users”
* add engagement-driven UI (gamification, delight, confetti)

Search is the interface.

---

# 6) We refuse decorative branding inside the product

We will never:

* use motifs, icons, or repeated marks as decoration
* use gradients inside the product UI
* stamp the logo everywhere
* use color to add “personality”

Brand presence is minimal. Authority comes from behavior.

---

# 7) We refuse discounts-as-strategy

We will never:

* chase growth by lowering seriousness
* discount aggressively to close deals
* position Celeste as “cheap” or “easy to try”

If a buyer is price-led, they are likely not a fit.

---

# 8) We refuse to be “nice” at the expense of clarity

We will never:

* soften language to be polite
* add fluff to avoid discomfort
* hide risk behind friendly copy

Celeste is calm, not comforting.

---

# 9) We refuse to build for owners as primary users

We will never:

* design the product to impress non-operators
* prioritize “pretty dashboards” over operational clarity
* let marketing optics override engineer reality

Engineers are the truth-makers. Always.

---

# 10) We refuse split-brain systems

We will never:

* position Celeste as an overlay to legacy PMS
* accept workflows that push users back into IDEA/AMOS/VOLY
* create two sources of truth

Legacy systems become data sources and export targets only.

Celeste is the work surface and system of record.

---

# 11) The kill switch test

If someone proposes something and it feels:

* faster but less explicit
* smarter but less accountable
* nicer but less serious
* modern but less timeless

Reject it.

---
